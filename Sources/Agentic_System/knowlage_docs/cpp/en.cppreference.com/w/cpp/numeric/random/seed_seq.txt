seed_seq::seed_seq seed_seq::generate seed_seq::size seed_seq::param [edit] Defined in header <random> class seed_seq ; (since C++11) std::seed_seq consumes a sequence of integer-valued data and produces a requested number of 32-bit unsigned integer values, based on the consumed data. The produced values are distributed over the entire 32-bit range even if the consumed values are close. It provides a way to seed a large number of random number engines or to seed a generator that requires a lot of entropy, given a small seed or a poorly distributed initial seed sequence. std::seed_seq meets the requirements of SeedSequence .
